HR Salary Negotiation — TCS

The negotiation-round playbook that pairs with the cram sheet. TCS is band-driven: you negotiate within a grade and escalate by justifying a higher grade — not by demanding a bigger number.

1 Reality check

  • Offers map to an internal grade. A 12+ yr lateral typically lands C3B / C4.
  • HR has a fixed band range and limited discretion — pushing past the ceiling rarely works.
  • Lateral offers anchor on your current CTC + a "% hike" (commonly 20–40% on a switch).
  • Joining bonus, relocation, notice-period buyout are often more flexible than base.
  • Current CTC and offer details get verified at onboarding — never inflate.
The single biggest lever: a one-grade bump (C3B → C4) is worth far more than haggling within a band. Spend your energy justifying the higher grade, not a higher number inside the same one.

4 Core scripts

"What is your current CTC?"

"My current fixed is ₹X with variable ₹Y, total ~₹Z. Moving into a more senior MLOps ownership role, I'm looking at ₹A–B, but I'm open to discussing the full package."

"What are your expectations?"

"Based on 12+ years, my AWS migration and LLMOps work, and market benchmarks for senior MLOps roles, I'm targeting ₹[top of range]. I'm flexible on structure — base, joining bonus, or variable — if the total is fair."

Lowball / "this is the band ceiling"

"I understand TCS has structured bands. Could we look at whether my experience justifies the next grade up? Multi-cloud Kubernetes, ~50–80% cost optimization, and end-to-end MLOps ownership map closer to Consultant scope than Associate."

If firm: "If base is capped, can we discuss a joining bonus or an accelerated 6-month review to bridge the gap?"

Notice period

"My notice is X days — I may be able to negotiate early release. Does TCS support a notice-period buyout?"

5 Direct account assignment + live phone call

You're hired against a specific billable client role (not bench), and the call may be unscheduled. Their urgency to fill the seat is your leverage.

Why it's leverage

  • TCS has a seat a client is waiting on — losing you is costlier than losing a generic bench hire.
  • Don't give away an early joining date for free — trade it for a better number.
  • Pocket line: "I'm interested and I know the account needs someone quickly — I can prioritize an early start if we align on a fair package."

Phone-call rules

  • Never get cornered into a snap number. Buy time: "I'd rather give a considered number than a rushed one — can I call you back by end of day?" Costs nothing.
  • Keep your cheat sheet on the desk now — current CTC, anchor ₹42–45 L, private floor. The call can come anytime; on the phone you can read it.
  • Use silence — state your number, then stop talking. Silence pressures them more on a call.
  • Get it in writing before committing — "Can you send the formal offer with the fixed/variable breakup?" Verbal phone offers are non-binding.
  • Don't verbally accept on the first call — "I'm excited — let me see the written offer and I'll come back quickly."
  • Confirm terms back before hanging up: "So — C4 band, ₹X fixed, ₹Y variable, joining by [date]?"

6 Likely HR questions

  • Why TCS / why leave? — frame as seeking larger MLOps ownership and scale; never badmouth current employer.
  • Expected vs. current CTC? — give a researched range, pivot to scope.
  • Notice period & earliest joining date? — honest, with buyout option.
  • Open to relocation / shifts / client location? — TCS values flexibility.
  • Strengths and one real weakness? — real weakness + concrete improvement step.
  • 5-year vision? — architect / principal MLOps track.

7 Pre-call checklist

On a sticky note on your desk, right now:
  • Current CTC (fixed + variable + total)
  • Anchor: ₹42–45 L
  • Private floor: current + 25–30% (never say aloud)
  • Reminders: ask grade (push C4) · ask fixed/variable split · get offer in writing · don't accept on call 1
